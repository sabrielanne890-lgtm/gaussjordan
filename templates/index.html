Gauss-Jordan Elimination

A Complete Method for Solving Systems of Linear Equations

Mathematical Discussion

Gauss-Jordan elimination is a method for solving systems of linear equations by transforming the augmented matrix into Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF).

The elementary row operations are:

  • Swap two rows
  • Multiply a row by a non-zero constant
  • Add a multiple of one row to another
$$[A \mid b] \rightarrow [I \mid x]$$

Example 1

$$ \begin{cases} 2x + y - z = 8 \\ -3x - y + 2z = -11 \\ -2x + y + 2z = -3 \end{cases} $$
$$ \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 2 & 1 & -1 & 8 \\ -3 & -1 & 2 & -11 \\ -2 & 1 & 2 & -3 \end{array} \right] $$

Final Answer

$$x = 2,\quad y = 3,\quad z = -1$$

Example 2

$$ \begin{cases} x + 2y + 3z = 9 \\ 2x - y + z = 8 \\ 3x - z = 3 \end{cases} $$
$$ \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 2 & 3 & 9 \\ 2 & -1 & 1 & 8 \\ 3 & 0 & -1 & 3 \end{array} \right] $$

Final Answer

$$x = 2,\quad y = -1,\quad z = 3$$
